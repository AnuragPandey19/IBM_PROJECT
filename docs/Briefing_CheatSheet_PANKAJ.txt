S9 — FUSION OF MODEL (HONEST NULL)
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Say (frame the honesty):
  "We combined Stage 1 output with Stage 2 embeddings via a small
   MLP. Empirically on IEEE-CIS, fusion matches Stage 1 alone —
   it does NOT improve over it. We report this honestly."

Head-to-head (TEST PR-AUC):
  - Stage 1 alone (LightGBM)        0.5036   ← Winner
  - Stage 2 alone (GraphSAGE)       0.4336
  - Fusion (LGBM + GNN embeddings)  0.4845   ← Slightly below Stage 1

Why fusion did not help (say this):
  "Stage 1's engineered velocity features plus target-encoded card1
   identity already capture most of the relational signal that
   GraphSAGE learns from the card1 subgraph. There is limited
   independent information to fuse."

Then say:
  "On a dataset with richer multi-hop patterns — money mule networks,
   cyclic transactions across accounts — fusion would likely help
   more. This is exactly the kind of honest three-axis evaluation
   our approach advocates: we report what actually happened."

S16 — ISOTONIC CALIBRATION
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Say (why calibration matters):
  - "A raw LightGBM score of 0.7 does NOT literally mean 70% chance
     of fraud."
  - "For risk-based routing — approve / review / block — we need
     meaningful probabilities."
  - "PSD2 and RBI expect thresholded decisions to be defensible.
     That requires calibrated scores."

Say (isotonic vs Platt):
  - "Platt fits a sigmoid — only one shape, symmetric."
  - "Isotonic fits any monotone step function — flexible enough for
     tree-ensemble score shapes."
  - "On our Sparkov val set: Brier 0.0016, ECE 0.0013 — well within
     the trustworthy range."

Post-retrain final numbers to know:
  - Test Brier: 0.0008
  - Test ECE:   0.0006

Point at the reliability diagram:  "Navy line — uncalibrated LightGBM,
sags below the diagonal at higher probabilities. Gold line — isotonic
recalibrated, hugs the diagonal closely."

Q&A DOMAIN — YOU handle:
  - Anything model-specific: LightGBM vs XGBoost, why leaf-wise growth,
    scale_pos_weight math, feature engineering choices, target encoding
    vs one-hot, isotonic vs Platt calibration.
  - "Why not deep learning?"  → Latency (<5 ms), interpretability
    (SHAP > attention weights for a business user), and tabular
    performance (trees are the sweet spot per Thivaios 2025).
  - "How did you handle class imbalance?"  → scale_pos_weight ~ 400,
    never SMOTE. Cost-sensitive at the loss level.
  - "What's the fusion result mean?"  → An honest null. Stage 1 alone
    beats fusion on IEEE-CIS because velocity + target encoding already
    capture the graph signal from card1 subgraph.
  - "Cross-dataset generalisation?"  → Same methodology, two datasets:
    IEEE-CIS baseline vs Sparkov post-retrain 0.805 test PR-AUC.
    Two of top-10 Sparkov features are engineered velocity — same type
    that dominated IEEE-CIS.
  - "Why augmented, not fine-tuned?"  → Fine-tuning would leak test
    cases into the model. Augmentation is training-time synthetic
    data that the model never sees at test time.
